Testinomial.

Josie is 10-years-old, studying three years above her grade level in the Program. In fact, she's studying the same math material that her mom, who is an eighth grade teacher, teaches her students. Imagine that, a 10-year-old transforming equations, working with linear functions and simplifying monomials and polynomials. She's well on her way to advanced algebraic expressions and she isn't even in middle school yet.

One of the things that Josie likes most about is the fact that she's studying above her grade level.

It gives me confidence because I know how strong of a student it has made me, says Josie. I know that I can do and learn anything if I do the work and practice daily. It has taught me about the importance of focusing and to not be afraid of challenges, but to enjoy them and work through them because I know I can overcome anything.

Josie embodies everything that Toru had in mind when he founded the Method. He felt that students would experience the true benefits of the Method if they acquired the study habits necessary for self-learning and advanced study.
